What is Eugenics?

From Wikipedia: Eugenics is a set of beliefs and practices that aim to improve the genetic quality of a human population by excluding certain genetic groups judged to be inferior, and promoting other genetic groups judged to be superior. Britannica.com has a similar definition: the selection of desired heritable characteristics in order to improve future generations, typically in reference to humans.

The History Behind Eugenics

History.com wrote an article about the influence of Eugenics. To summarize, Plato, a Greek philosopher, noted the idea about "creating a society by procreating high-class people together and discouraging coupling between the lower classes...and incentuous relationships between parents and children were forbidden but not between brother and sister." This idea of creating a perfect society created the notion for eugenics and set way for other people to think alike.

Charles Darwin, another eugenist, believed to better humankind through propagation of the British elite; however, his ideas never spread in his own country, it spread in America. In 1896 Connecticut first made it illegal to marry someone who has epilepsy or who was "feeble-minded." Another example of American eugenics is John Harvey Kellogg, the inventor of the Kellogg cereal, who created and organized the Race Betterment Foundation, which led conferences on eugenics.

As we can see, throughout history there has been development on this idea of eugenics and creating a society that leads towards perfection, so how does that affect us today? Through genetic engineering we are creating babies who are born to fit specific needs, those who are genetically modified. So what exactly is a negative or positive trait? There is no clear distinction and genetic engineering may as well be considered eugenics, today.
