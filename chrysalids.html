Eugenics and the Chrysalids



The idea of eugenics in the Chrysalids is heavily emphasized through religion. Through the thoughts of the main character, David, we are able to see what the Waknuk society believes in. In this case, mutants, those that look different, are sometimes killed, sterilized, and sent out of the area to die. These people are casted out to die, of starvation or what we can infer to be nuclear radiation. Keeping society "pure" is the main idea in the Waknuk society.


To take this into perspective, lets analyze Adolf Hitler. In the video below, we are presented multiple speeches that Hitler gave out throughout his life. To summarize, Hitler believed in purity, that there can be only one race and to quote "Instill in the minds of our youth the knowledge that the jews will be the destruction of mankind." Similar to the Waknuk belief that all abnormalities should be sterilized and sent out to the fringes, Hitler also believed in purifying his race by mass genocide, in which one way was to unleash toxic gas so that everyone in the surrounding area would die.


Towards the end of the story we meet a lady, a Sealander, that wants David to keep Petra safe at all costs because of her power. When David and the Sealander finally meet, we are told that everyone who was fighting, people in the fringes and the Waknuk society, are suffocated to death by this weird substance that cocoons people. Comparing this to Hitler's mass genocide, "...The place was festooned with threads, which appeared more white than glossy now: it took a moment or two of feeling something was wrong with them before one perceived that they failed to move in the breeze as webs would. And not only they, but everything was motionless, petrified." The Sealander states that anyone that comes between her race and perfection, dies. She also mentions that someone else will come in later and probably wipe out her society, but for now she just needs Petra to continue succeeding in her society.

This poses the question, will Petra be breeded to produce more of her kind (super advanced human being)? Although traditional breeding is not considered genetic engineering, in a way the Sealander is breeding to better her society; to advance it and to bring purity among the telepathic people.
